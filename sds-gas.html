2. Hazard Identification

GHS Classification: Not flammable/combustible by OSHA or GHS criteria (flash point 203°C, confirmed by lab testing; consistent with the Bel-Ray® 2T oil component, itself rated not flammable/combustible at 98°C). Skin Irritant Cat. 2 and Eye Irritant Cat. 2 — driven primarily by the Bel-Ray® 2T oil at 50% concentration (its own supplier SDS rates it OSHA-hazardous, eye- and skin-irritating), with additional contribution from ZDDP. Hazardous to the Aquatic Environment — likely Long-Term Cat. 2 rather than Cat. 3, given the Stoddard Solvent sub-component's high bioaccumulation potential (log Kow 3.16–7.15) within the Bel-Ray oil. Confirm exact aquatic category and any CNS/narcosis-related STOT statements with a regulatory chemist before finalizing.

Signal Word: Warning

  • H411 (Aquatic Chronic 2) or H413 (Aquatic Chronic 3) — pending confirmation of exact category
  • Possible STOT-repeated exposure statement (CNS/narcosis) — Bel-Ray supplier SDS notes prolonged inhalation may cause central nervous system effects; confirm whether this carries through to the finished blend

4. First-Aid Measures

Inhalation: Move to fresh air. Call a physician if symptoms develop or persist. Prolonged inhalation may be harmful (CNS effects per Bel-Ray® component supplier data).

Skin Contact: Remove and isolate contaminated clothing and shoes. Wash off immediately with plenty of water and soap. Get medical attention if irritation develops or persists.

Eye Contact: Immediately flush eyes with plenty of water for at least 15 minutes, holding eyelids open. Get medical attention if irritation develops or persists.

Ingestion: Rinse mouth thoroughly. Do not induce vomiting. If a large amount is swallowed, call a poison control center immediately. Never give liquid to an unconscious person.

Notes to physician: Symptoms may be delayed. Ensure medical personnel are aware of the materials involved and take precautions to protect themselves.

5. Fire-Fighting Measures

Suitable Extinguishing Media: Water, water fog, foam, dry chemical powder, carbon dioxide (CO2).

Specific Hazards: Flash point 203°C (lab-confirmed) — not flammable/combustible by OSHA or GHS criteria. Move containers from fire area if safe to do so; cool exposed containers with water spray. Hazardous combustion products may include nitrogen oxides, carbon monoxide, and carbon dioxide.

6. Accidental Release Measures

Keep unnecessary personnel away and upwind of the spill. Wear appropriate protective equipment and avoid skin/eye contact. Beware of slippery surfaces from spilled material. Prevent further leakage/spillage if safe to do so; do not contaminate water. Absorb with liquid-binding material (sand, diatomite, sawdust). Recover spilled product and place in suitable closed containers for disposal per Section 13. Never return spills to original containers for reuse.

7. Handling and Storage

Store at room temperature, in a cool, well-ventilated place away from heat, sparks, and open flame. Protect material from direct sunlight. Avoid contact with skin and eyes. Do not use in areas without adequate ventilation. Avoid prolonged exposure. Wash thoroughly after handling. Avoid release to the environment. Note: hydrogen sulfide (H2S) may be released if this material is heated — do not depend on sense of smell for warning. Keep away from food, drink, and animal feedstuffs. Keep out of reach of children.

8. Exposure Controls / Personal Protection

Exposure Limits: Bel-Ray® 2T oil components — base oils 5 mg/m³ TWA (PEL/mist), Antimony Tris(dipentylcarbamodithioato) 0.5 mg/m³ TWA, Stoddard Solvent 100 ppm TWA (ACGIH) / 2900 mg/m³ (500 ppm) PEL. ZDDP component — no established workplace exposure limit per supplier data.

Personal Protective Equipment: Avoid contact with eyes and skin — wear suitable protective clothing and impervious gloves. Good general ventilation (10+ air changes/hour recommended) or local exhaust ventilation to maintain airborne levels below exposure limits. Certified respirator required if workers face concentrations above exposure limits.

10. Stability and Reactivity

Stable under normal temperatures and pressures when stored and used as directed. Avoid heat, sparks, and open flame. Avoid strong oxidizing agents (incompatible with ZDDP component). No hazardous polymerization. Thermal decomposition may release phosphine, nitrogen oxides, sulfur oxides, toxic metal oxide fumes, and hydrogen sulfide (per Bel-Ray® 2T oil component supplier data) — do not depend on odor for warning of H2S release.

11. Toxicological Information

Causes eye irritation and is irritating to skin (driven by the Bel-Ray® 2T oil at 50% concentration and the ZDDP component). May be irritating to inhale; prolonged inhalation may be harmful and is associated with central nervous system effects (narcosis, decreased motor function, behavioral changes) per the Bel-Ray® component supplier SDS. Ingestion may cause gastrointestinal irritation, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Repeated absorption may affect the central nervous system, liver, kidneys, and blood per supplier data. Not considered a carcinogen by IARC, ACGIH, NTP, or OSHA based on available component data.

12. Ecological Information

Components of this product are hazardous to aquatic life and may cause long-term adverse effects in the environment, attributable to both the ZDDP component and the Stoddard Solvent/antimony sub-components within the Bel-Ray® 2T oil (log Kow 3.16–7.15, indicating bioaccumulation potential). Avoid release to the environment, drains, or waterways.

13. Disposal Considerations

This product, in its present state, is not expected to be a hazardous waste per the Bel-Ray® 2T oil component's supplier classification (40 CFR 261.4(b)(4)), though the ZDDP component's irritant classification should be considered. Dispose of in accordance with local, regional, and national regulations. Do not pour into drains or waterways. It is the responsibility of the user to determine, at time of disposal, whether the product meets hazardous waste criteria in their jurisdiction.
