4. First-Aid Measures

Inhalation: Move to fresh air. Seek medical attention if breathing becomes difficult or symptoms persist.

Skin Contact: Remove contaminated clothing. Wash affected area immediately with plenty of soap and water. Get medical attention if irritation develops or persists.

Eye Contact: Immediately flush eyes with plenty of water for at least 15 minutes, holding eyelids open. Remove contact lenses if present and easy to do. Continue rinsing. Get medical attention if irritation develops or persists.

Ingestion: Rinse mouth with water. Do not induce vomiting unless directed to do so by medical personnel. Drink water to dilute. Never give anything by mouth to an unconscious person. Seek immediate medical attention.

5. Fire-Fighting Measures

Suitable Extinguishing Media: Foam, dry chemical powder, carbon dioxide (CO2). Use water spray to cool containers exposed to fire.

Specific Hazards: Flash point 200°C (lab-confirmed) — not classified as a flammable liquid under GHS, but combustible at sustained high heat. May release toxic gases (phosphorus oxides, sulfur oxides, carbon monoxide/dioxide) if heated to decomposition or involved in a fire.

6. Accidental Release Measures

Eliminate ignition sources in the area. Wear appropriate protective equipment (gloves, eye protection) and avoid skin/eye contact with spilled material. Contain spill and absorb with an inert liquid-binding material (sand, diatomaceous earth, sawdust). Avoid release to drains, surface water, or groundwater. Collect into suitable closed containers for disposal per Section 13. Wash the spill area with soap and water after removal.

7. Handling and Storage

Store at room temperature (15–25°C recommended), in a cool, dry, well-ventilated location away from direct sunlight, heat, and ignition sources. Use only with adequate general ventilation. Avoid contact with skin and eyes; avoid breathing mist or vapor. Keep container tightly closed when not in use. Keep away from food, drink, and animal feedstuffs. Keep out of reach of children. Avoid storage near oxidizing agents.

8. Exposure Controls / Personal Protection

Exposure Limits: ZDDP component — no established workplace exposure limit per supplier data. Klotz® base component — mineral oil mist exposure limit 5 mg/m³ TWA (OSHA Table Z-1).

Personal Protective Equipment: Eye protection (safety glasses or goggles) and impervious gloves recommended when handling. Use in a well-ventilated area; respiratory protection generally not required under normal use, but a NIOSH-approved respirator may be advisable if aerosol or mist is generated. Wash hands after handling and before breaks.

10. Stability and Reactivity

Stable under normal temperatures and pressures when stored and used as directed. Avoid heat, sparks, and open flame. Avoid contact with strong oxidizing agents (incompatible). No hazardous polymerization. Thermal decomposition may release phosphine, nitrogen oxides, sulfur oxides, and toxic metal oxide fumes (from the ZDDP component) — see Section 5.

11. Toxicological Information

Causes skin irritation and serious eye irritation (driven primarily by the ZDDP anti-wear component). Ingestion may cause gastrointestinal irritation, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Prolonged or repeated skin contact may cause dermal irritation. Not classified as a carcinogen, germ cell mutagen, or reproductive toxicant based on available component data. Component oral LD50 (ZDDP raw material) is not classified as acutely toxic per supplier data.

12. Ecological Information

Hazardous to aquatic life with long-lasting effects (Aquatic Chronic Cat. 3), attributable to the ZDDP component. Avoid release to the environment, drains, or waterways. Persistence, degradability, and bioaccumulation data for the finished blend are not available; component-level data should be requested from suppliers for a complete assessment.

13. Disposal Considerations

Dispose of in accordance with local, regional, and national regulations. Do not pour into drains or waterways. Offer surplus/unused product to a licensed waste disposal service. Dispose of contaminated packaging as unused product, or recycle if rinsed and empty.

14. Transport Information

UN Number: Not regulated as dangerous goods (not flammable, flash point 200°C).

Proper Shipping Name: Not applicable — not regulated.

Transport Hazard Class: Not regulated (DOT, IMDG, IATA).

15. Regulatory Information

This product is considered a hazardous chemical under OSHA Hazard Communication Standard (29 CFR 1910.1200) due to the ZDDP component's skin/eye irritant classification. TSCA, REACH, and other regional chemical inventory status for the finished blend not yet confirmed — component suppliers (Klotz, Rislone) list their materials as TSCA-listed or exempt; verify finished-product status with a regulatory professional before international distribution.
