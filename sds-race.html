4. First-Aid Measures

Inhalation: Provide fresh air immediately. If breathing is difficult or symptoms develop, seek medical attention. Nausea and headache are possible from nitromethane component exposure.

Skin Contact: Remove contaminated clothing immediately. Wash affected area thoroughly with soap and water. Get medical attention if irritation develops or persists.

Eye Contact: Immediately flush eyes with plenty of water for at least 15 minutes, holding eyelids open. Remove contact lenses if present and easy to do. Continue rinsing. Seek medical attention immediately — this product causes serious eye irritation.

Ingestion: Rinse mouth with water (only if conscious). Do not induce vomiting. Seek medical attention immediately. Nitromethane component may cause diarrhea, nausea, and vomiting if swallowed.

Indication of immediate medical attention: Symptoms (including methaemoglobinaemia, liver/kidney effects) may be delayed following nitromethane exposure — show this SDS to attending medical personnel.

5. Fire-Fighting Measures

Suitable Extinguishing Media: Water spray, alcohol-resistant foam, dry chemical powder, BC-powder, carbon dioxide (CO2). Unsuitable: Water jet (may spread fire).

Specific Hazards: Flammable liquid, flash point 35°C (lab-confirmed), GHS Flammable Liquid Cat. 3 — combustible and can be ignited by ignition sources. Vapors are heavier than air and may spread along floors, collecting in low/unventilated areas. May form flammable/explosive vapor-air mixtures in poorly ventilated spaces. Hazardous combustion products may include nitrogen oxides, carbon monoxide, and carbon dioxide. Firefighters should wear self-contained breathing apparatus and fight fire from a safe distance.

6. Accidental Release Measures

Eliminate all ignition sources (no smoking, sparks, or open flame) before approaching a spill. Wear appropriate protective equipment and avoid skin/eye contact. Ventilate the affected area. Contain spill (cover drains) and absorb with an inert liquid-binding material (sand, diatomaceous earth, acid- or universal binders). Avoid release to drains, surface water, or groundwater — notify downstream users of possible contamination. Collect into suitable closed containers for disposal per Section 13.

7. Handling and Storage

Store at room temperature (15–25°C recommended), in a cool, dry, well-ventilated location away from direct sunlight, heat, and all ignition sources (no smoking, sparks, or open flame) due to the nitromethane component's flammability. Take precautionary measures against static discharge; ground/bond containers and receiving equipment. Use only with adequate ventilation — avoid unventilated below-ground storage areas where heavier-than-air vapors could collect. Keep container tightly closed when not in use. Keep away from food, drink, and animal feedstuffs. Keep out of reach of children. Avoid storage near oxidizing agents and the other incompatible materials listed in Section 10.

8. Exposure Controls / Personal Protection

Exposure Limits: Nitromethane (CAS 75-52-5) — UK WEL: TWA 100 ppm (254 mg/m³), STEL 150 ppm (381 mg/m³). ZDDP component — no established workplace exposure limit per supplier data. Klotz® base component — mineral oil mist 5 mg/m³ TWA (OSHA Table Z-1).

Personal Protective Equipment: Safety goggles with side protection required (eye damage/irritation hazard). Chemical-resistant gloves tested to EN 374 recommended; butyl rubber gloves (≥0.5mm thickness) provide >480 minute breakthrough time against nitromethane per supplier data. Use only in well-ventilated areas; respiratory protection (Type A organic vapor cartridge respirator) recommended if aerosol or vapor exposure is expected. Wash hands before breaks and after handling.

10. Stability and Reactivity

Reactive substance due to the nitromethane component — risk of ignition if heated; vapors may form explosive mixtures with air. Stable under normal ambient storage/handling temperature and pressure. Incompatible materials: acids, bases, strong oxidizers, acetone, aldehydes, alkali hydroxides, amines, ammonia, halogenated hydrocarbons, metal powders, methanol, perchlorates — violent/explosive reaction possible with these. Keep away from heat, sparks, open flame. Avoid strong oxidizing agents (also incompatible with ZDDP component). No hazardous polymerization. Thermal decomposition may release nitrogen oxides, carbon monoxide/dioxide, phosphorus oxides, and sulfur oxides.

11. Toxicological Information

Acute toxicity (nitromethane component): Oral LD50 1,478 mg/kg (rat); Dermal LD50 >2,000 mg/kg (rabbit); harmful if swallowed or inhaled. Carcinogenicity: Suspected of causing cancer (Carc. Cat. 2) — from nitromethane at 8% concentration, well above the 0.1% generic classification cutoff. Reproductive toxicity: Suspected of damaging fertility and the unborn child (Repr. Cat. 2). Skin/eye: Causes skin irritation and serious eye irritation (ZDDP and Klotz base components). Other effects: Liver and kidney damage, methaemoglobinaemia reported for nitromethane exposure per supplier data. Symptoms may be delayed.

12. Ecological Information

Hazardous to aquatic life with long-lasting effects (Aquatic Chronic Cat. 3), attributable to the ZDDP component. Nitromethane component itself is not classified as hazardous to the aquatic environment per supplier data (fish LC50 >659 mg/l, 96h). Theoretical degradation of nitromethane component: ~10% biotic/abiotic over 28 days (not readily biodegradable). Avoid release to the environment, drains, or waterways.

13. Disposal Considerations

This material and its container must be disposed of as hazardous waste due to the nitromethane component (flammable, carcinogen, reproductive toxicant). Dispose of in accordance with local, regional, and national regulations — do not pour into drains or waterways. Only approved hazardous-waste packaging may be used for disposal. Non-contaminated, rinsed packaging may be recycled.
